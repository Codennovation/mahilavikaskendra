About Mahila Vikas Kendra

History

Take A Look at How We Begin

Mahila Vikas Kendra was founded in 1996 by a group of well-educated and successful women who wanted to uplift the social and financial status of the women in society.

Learn More

We are a Mumbai based NGO focused women empowerment.

Our focus has been to build employable skills of less-educated and under-privilege women so that they can find gainful employment to sustain themselves with dignity

All our vocational courses cater to current market demands such as Bedside Patient Assistant Course, Tailoring, Beautician Course, etc.

Issue

Lack of access to sustainable livelihood opportunities for less educated and under-privileged women

Training/Vocational courses are not market-linked

Low quality of education curriculum due to lack of involvement of industry in course development.

Market-Demand

Current market demand is not considered while offering training/vocational courses.

Lack of access and knowledge of existing opportunities

Job exchanges have limited opportunities and are dysfunctional.

Lack of employable/entrepreneurship skills

90% of women drop out of education system before 10th grade.

Unsustainable income at work

70% of urban population specially women are engaged in informal work which is intermittent is nature. Most of these women are not covered under any laws

Minimum Wages

The minimum wages in India are not linked to latest inflation rate and informal workers are paid far less than minimum wages stipulated by government.
